🖊️ My Journal

Keeping a journal when you have bipolar isn’t just helpful — it’s powerful. Writing down your thoughts, feelings, triggers, and wins creates a safe space to process what you're going through. It helps you recognize patterns in your moods, track early warning signs, and gain insight into what helps or hurts your stability. On tough days, it can be a quiet companion, and on better days, it can remind you of your strength. Journaling can be an act of self-care, self-discovery, and healing — all on your terms, in your words. And best of all? It’s just for you.
